Type a novel or a single space. It leaves as 160 bits.

SHA-1 crushes any input — one byte or a gigabyte — into a fixed 40-hex fingerprint, and there's no way back. The message is padded to a multiple of 512 bits, cut into blocks, and stirred through 80 rounds that mix five 32-bit registers. Change one letter below and watch roughly half the output flip.
